üîç Research Strategies

‚úÖ Effective Research Tips

  • Start with a research question: Formulate a clear, focused question to guide your search
  • Use academic databases: Google Scholar, JSTOR, PubMed, ERIC, and your library's databases
  • Evaluate sources critically: Check author credentials, publication date, and publisher reputation
  • Take organized notes: Use citation management software or spreadsheets to track sources
  • Look for recent sources: Generally, use sources from the last 5-10 years for current topics

üìö Database Search Strategies

  • Boolean operators: Use AND, OR, NOT to refine searches (e.g., "climate change AND policy")
  • Truncation: Use * to find variations (e.g., "educat*" finds educate, education, educator)
  • Phrase searching: Use quotes for exact phrases (e.g., "artificial intelligence")
  • Field searching: Limit to title, abstract, or author fields
  • Citation chaining: Check references in good articles to find related sources

‚úÖ Effective Writing Strategies

  • Write daily: Even 30 minutes daily is better than one long session
  • Start with the easiest section: Build momentum by writing what you know best
  • Write first, edit later: Don't try to perfect each sentence as you write
  • Use headings and subheadings: Organize your thoughts and guide the reader
  • Take breaks: Use Pomodoro technique (25 min work, 5 min break)

‚ùå Common Writing Mistakes to Avoid

  • Writing the introduction first (often easier to write it last)
  • Trying to sound "academic" with overly complex language
  • Not leaving enough time for revision and editing
  • Failing to connect each paragraph to your thesis
  • Using too many direct quotes instead of paraphrasing

üìã Citation & Formatting Tips

Best Practices for Citations

  • Cite as you write: Add citations immediately when using a source
  • Use citation management tools: Zotero, Mendeley, or our free generators
  • Check required style: Confirm which style (APA, MLA, Chicago) your institution requires
  • Be consistent: Use the same style throughout your paper
  • Verify accuracy: Double-check all citations against official style guides

‚úÖ Citation Checklist

  • All direct quotes have page numbers in citations
  • Reference list/Works Cited is alphabetized
  • All in-text citations appear in reference list
  • All reference entries are properly formatted
  • DOI or URL included where required
  • Hanging indents applied correctly
  • Consistent punctuation and capitalization

When to Cite vs. When Not to Cite

‚úÖ Cite These:
  • Direct quotes
  • Paraphrased ideas
  • Statistical data
  • Research findings
  • Images or graphs
  • Specific facts
‚ùå Don't Cite These:
  • Common knowledge
  • Your own ideas
  • Standard procedures
  • Generally accepted facts
  • Personal experiences
  • Original research/data

‚ö†Ô∏è Avoiding Plagiarism

‚ùå What Constitutes Plagiarism

  • Direct plagiarism: Copying text word-for-word without quotation marks or citation
  • Paraphrasing plagiarism: Changing a few words but keeping the same structure without citation
  • Mosaic plagiarism: Mixing copied phrases with original text without proper citation
  • Self-plagiarism: Reusing your own previously published work without permission
  • Accidental plagiarism: Forgetting to cite sources or improper paraphrasing

‚úÖ How to Avoid Plagiarism

  • Take careful notes: Clearly distinguish between quotes, paraphrases, and your ideas
  • Cite everything: When in doubt, cite the source
  • Use plagiarism checkers: Tools like Turnitin or Grammarly can help identify issues
  • Learn proper paraphrasing: Restate ideas in your own words and sentence structure
  • Keep track of sources: Maintain a working bibliography as you research

Proper Paraphrasing Techniques

Original Text: "Climate change is causing sea levels to rise at an unprecedented rate, threatening coastal communities worldwide."

‚ùå Poor Paraphrase (plagiarism): Climate change is making sea levels increase at a fast rate, endangering coastal communities globally.

‚úÖ Good Paraphrase: According to recent research, coastal populations face significant risks due to the rapid increase in sea levels caused by climate change (Smith, 2023).

üîç Editing & Proofreading Tips

The Editing Process

  1. Take a break: Step away from your paper for at least 24 hours
  2. Read aloud: You'll catch more errors when you hear the words
  3. Check structure: Ensure logical flow between paragraphs and sections
  4. Verify citations: Check all citations against the reference list
  5. Format check: Verify margins, spacing, headers, and page numbers
  6. Final proofread: Read backward (sentence by sentence) to catch typos

‚úÖ Common Errors to Check

  • Subject-verb agreement
  • Comma splices and run-on sentences
  • Apostrophe usage (it's vs. its)
  • Consistent tense throughout
  • Spelling of technical terms
  • Formatting consistency
  • Page numbers and headers
  • Reference list accuracy
